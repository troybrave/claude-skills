# Production Descriptors

Comprehensive reference for shaping the *sound* beyond genre.

---

## Texture Descriptors

### Clean/Polished
- `crisp` - Clear, defined, sharp edges
- `polished` - Professional, clean mix
- `pristine` - Ultra-clean, no grit
- `crystal clear` - Transparent, defined
- `slick` - Smooth, professional

### Warm/Analog
- `warm` - Rich low-mids, analog feel
- `vintage analog` - Retro equipment sound
- `tape saturation` - Subtle harmonic distortion
- `tube warmth` - Valve amplifier character
- `vinyl warmth` - Record player aesthetic

### Lo-Fi/Raw
- `lo-fi` - Intentionally degraded quality
- `raw` - Unpolished, authentic
- `gritty` - Rough texture, edge
- `dusty` - Aged, vintage samples
- `bedroom production` - DIY aesthetic

### Distorted/Heavy
- `distorted` - Overdriven, clipped
- `fuzzy` - Vintage distortion character
- `crunchy` - Mid-range distortion
- `heavy` - Thick, powerful
- `aggressive` - Intense, attacking

---

## Space/Room Descriptors

### Intimate
- `intimate` - Close, personal
- `bedroom` - Small room, DIY feel
- `close-mic` - Tight, present
- `dry` - Minimal reverb
- `in-your-ear` - Whisper-close

### Medium Space
- `room sound` - Natural ambience
- `live room` - Recording studio space
- `studio` - Controlled environment
- `wooden room` - Acoustic warmth

### Large Space
- `arena` - Large venue sound
- `stadium` - Massive space
- `cathedral reverb` - Long, spiritual echo
- `hall reverb` - Concert hall ambience
- `vast` - Enormous, expansive
- `spacious` - Open, airy

### Ethereal
- `atmospheric` - Ambient, textural
- `dreamy` - Hazy, floating
- `washed-out` - Heavy reverb/delay
- `underwater` - Muffled, deep
- `otherworldly` - Alien, unusual

---

## Mix Characteristics

### Frequency Balance
- `bass-heavy` - Emphasized low end
- `sub-bass` - Deep, rumbling lows
- `bright` - Emphasized highs
- `dark` - Rolled-off highs
- `mid-forward` - Emphasized mids
- `scooped mids` - V-shaped EQ

### Vocal Treatment
- `vocal-forward` - Voice prominent in mix
- `buried vocals` - Voice blended into mix
- `double-tracked vocals` - Layered lead vocal
- `layered harmonies` - Multiple vocal parts
- `processed vocals` - Effects on voice
- `raw vocals` - Unprocessed, natural

### Arrangement
- `stripped-back` - Minimal instrumentation
- `sparse` - Few elements, space
- `dense` - Many layers
- `layered` - Multiple textures
- `full arrangement` - Complete instrumentation
- `minimal` - Essential elements only

### Dynamics
- `compressed` - Even dynamics
- `dynamic` - Wide volume range
- `punchy` - Impactful transients
- `soft` - Gentle dynamics
- `building` - Increasing intensity
- `explosive` - Sudden dynamic changes

## Era/Aesthetic Markers

### Decades
- `50s` - Early rock and roll, doo-wop
- `60s` - British invasion, psychedelic
- `70s` - Disco, classic rock, funk
- `80s` - Synths, new wave, power ballads
- `90s` - Grunge, alternative, R&B
- `2000s` - Pop-punk, crunk, emo
- `2010s` - EDM, trap, indie
- `2020s` - Hyperpop, bedroom pop

### Production Eras
- `vintage` - Old recording techniques
- `retro` - Nostalgic, throwback
- `classic` - Timeless sound
- `modern` - Contemporary production
- `futuristic` - Forward-thinking
- `timeless` - Era-agnostic

### Technology
- `analog` - Pre-digital warmth
- `digital` - Clean, precise
- `hybrid` - Mixed approach
- `sampled` - Using recordings
- `live` - Performed, not programmed

## Mood/Energy Modifiers

### Energy Level
- `high-energy` - Intense, driving
- `mid-tempo` - Moderate pace
- `laid-back` - Relaxed, easy
- `chill` - Calm, mellow
- `explosive` - Bursting energy
- `building energy` - Increasing intensity

### Emotional Tone
- `uplifting` - Positive, inspiring
- `melancholic` - Sad, wistful
- `aggressive` - Intense, forceful
- `peaceful` - Calm, serene
- `dark` - Moody, brooding
- `euphoric` - Ecstatic, joyful
- `bittersweet` - Mixed emotions
- `nostalgic` - Longing, reminiscent
- `hopeful` - Optimistic
- `mysterious` - Enigmatic

## Tempo Descriptors

### Exact BPM (when helpful)
- `60-70 BPM` - Very slow, ballad
- `70-90 BPM` - Slow, relaxed
- `90-110 BPM` - Mid-tempo
- `110-125 BPM` - Moderate, danceable
- `125-140 BPM` - Energetic
- `140-160 BPM` - Fast, driving
- `160-180 BPM` - Very fast, punk/D&B

### Descriptive Tempo
- `slow tempo` - Leisurely pace
- `mid-tempo` - Moderate
- `up-tempo` - Faster than average
- `fast` - Quick pace
- `driving` - Propulsive rhythm
- `gentle tempo` - Soft, unhurried
- `breakneck` - Extremely fast
