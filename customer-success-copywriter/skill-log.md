## Core References

This skill draws from three books. Read relevant sections before each run.

### 1. Customer Success - Nick Mehta
**Location:** `/Users/troybrave/Documents/Projects/Full Vault/Business/01 - Reference/Books/Reference/Nick Mehta/Customer Success/Customer Success - Complete.md`

**Key Laws for Email Writing:**
- Law 7: Obsessively Improve Time-to-Value
- Law 8: Deeply Understand Customer Metrics
- Law 9: Drive Through Hard Metrics
- Law 10: Top-Down, Company-Wide Commitment

**Quick Reference:**
- Time-to-value = Metric that matters most in early lifecycle
- Health scores = Leading indicator of churn/expansion
- Net retention > 100% = Ultimate success measure
- Success measures must be concrete and customer-defined

### 2. Obviously Awesome - April Dunford
**Location:** `/Users/troybrave/Documents/Projects/Full Vault/Business/01 - Reference/Books/Reference/April Dunford/Obviously Awesome/Obviously Awesome - Complete.md`

**Key Frameworks for Email Writing:**
- 5-step positioning: Alternatives → Attributes → Value → Who Cares → Market Frame
- Feature → Benefit → Value translation
- "Big Fish, Small Pond" positioning
- Trend layering (optional)

**Quick Reference:**
- Always articulate value in customer terms, not product terms
- Lead with what alternatives lack, not just what you have
- "Who cares a lot" = Your best-fit customer segment
- Positioning drives every piece of messaging

### 3. Influence - Robert Cialdini
**Location:** `/Users/troybrave/Documents/Projects/Full Vault/Business/01 - Reference/Books/Reference/Robert Cialdini/Influence/Influence - The Psychology of Persuasion - Complete.md`

**6 Principles for Email Writing:**
1. **Reciprocation** - Give before asking
2. **Commitment/Consistency** - Reference past actions
3. **Social Proof** - What similar others do
4. **Liking** - Build genuine rapport
5. **Authority** - Data and expertise
6. **Scarcity** - Only when genuinely applicable

**Quick Reference:**
- Reciprocation is most powerful for expansion/referral asks
- Commitment/Consistency works for onboarding (small wins build)
- Social Proof requires similarity to be effective
- Liking requires genuine concern, not manipulation
- Scarcity backfires when used on at-risk customers
